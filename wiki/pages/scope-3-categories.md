**In short:** The [[ghg-protocol|GHG Protocol]] divides [[scope-3-emissions|Scope 3]] into 15 numbered categories, eight upstream (things you buy) and seven downstream (what happens to what you sell). You do not need all 15; a handful dominate for drinks.

## Upstream categories (1 to 8)

1. **Purchased goods and services**: everything you buy to make and run your business, including ingredients, glass, cans, labels, cardboard and contract services. Almost always the biggest category for drinks.
2. **Capital goods**: long-life equipment you buy, such as tanks, stills, bottling lines and buildings, spread over their purchase year.
3. **Fuel- and energy-related activities**: the "hidden" emissions behind your energy, such as extracting and refining the fuel before you burn it, and grid losses. A modest but standard add-on to [[scope-1-emissions|Scope 1]] and [[scope-2-emissions|Scope 2]].
4. **Upstream transportation and distribution**: freight you pay for, inbound and outbound, plus third-party warehousing.
5. **Waste generated in operations**: treating and disposing of your site waste, including spent grain, pomace and wastewater.
6. **Business travel**: flights, trains and hotel stays for work trips.
7. **Employee commuting**: staff getting to and from work, plus home working energy if you include it.
8. **Upstream leased assets**: emissions from things you lease but do not count in Scopes 1 and 2, such as a rented warehouse outside your boundary.

## Downstream categories (9 to 15)

9. **Downstream transportation and distribution**: freight and storage you do not pay for, such as a distributor moving your stock at their cost.
10. **Processing of sold products**: a customer processing your product further, for example a bulk spirit you sell that someone else bottles.
11. **Use of sold products**: emissions from using what you sell. Drinks need no fuel to drink, but chilling and ice at bars, retail and home are often estimated here.
12. **End-of-life treatment of sold products**: recycling, landfill or incineration of your packaging after use. Links closely to [[recycled-content]] and [[extended-producer-responsibility]].
13. **Downstream leased assets**: assets you own and lease to others.
14. **Franchises**: emissions of franchise operations running under your brand.
15. **Investments**: emissions financed by your investments; mainly relevant to banks and investors.

## What this means for drinks businesses

- For most producers and brands, categories 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 11 and 12 cover nearly everything, with category 1 alone often more than half. See [[drinks-carbon-hotspots]].
- Categories 8, 10, 13, 14 and 15 are often genuinely irrelevant. The standard lets you exclude categories that do not apply, but you must say which and why.
- Screen all 15 roughly first with [[emission-factors|spend-based estimates]], then invest in better [[primary-vs-secondary-data|primary data]] only where the numbers are big.
- The Scope 3 Standard is being revised, with a consultation draft expected during 2026 and a final edition around the end of 2027. Category definitions and reporting rules may change, so check current guidance before locking in a reporting format.
