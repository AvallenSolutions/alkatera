**In short:** The functional unit is the "per what" of a [[life-cycle-assessment]]: the defined quantity of product or service that all the results are measured against, for example one litre of packaged beer delivered to a customer. Without it, footprint numbers cannot be compared.

## Why it matters

An LCA or [[product-carbon-footprint]] produces results like "kilograms of CO2e per functional unit". Change the unit and the number changes with it: per 330 ml can, per 700 ml bottle, per litre, per serving. None of these is wrong, but comparing a per-serving figure for one product with a per-litre figure for another is meaningless, and doing so in marketing is a fast route to [[greenwashing]].

The functional unit should describe the function delivered, not just an amount of stuff. "One litre of packaged drink, ready for sale" captures the liquid, its packaging and its readiness; "one bottle" does not say what size or whether the liquid inside counts.

## Choosing one for drinks

- **Per litre of packaged product** is the most common choice for producers, because it lets different pack formats be compared on a like-for-like basis.
- **Per container** (bottle, can, keg) is intuitive for consumers and useful on labels, but always state the size.
- **Per serving** can be fairer when products are consumed differently, for example comparing a spirit measure with a beer, though serving definitions vary and should be declared.

Whatever you choose, pair it with clearly stated [[system-boundaries]]: a per-litre figure to the factory gate is not comparable with a per-litre figure that includes retail and disposal.

## What this means for drinks businesses

State the functional unit and boundary next to every published figure. When comparing your own products, formats or recipes over time, keep the unit constant, and when a customer sends a questionnaire, check which unit they want before submitting; see also [[carbon-intensity]] for how per-unit figures relate to whole-company totals, and [[primary-vs-secondary-data]] for what drives the number's quality.
