**In short:** Sustainability reporting is a crowded landscape of laws, voluntary frameworks and customer questionnaires. For most drinks SMEs, none of the big laws applies directly; the requests arrive through customers instead, and the same core data answers nearly all of them.

## The mandatory layer

- **EU:** the [[csrd]] requires large companies to publish audited sustainability information using the [[esrs]] standards, built on [[double-materiality]]. Its scope has been narrowed and its timelines pushed back since it first became law, so as of mid-2026 it is settling on the largest companies. For SMEs the EU offers [[vsme]], a deliberately simple voluntary standard designed to answer the questions big customers and banks ask.
- **UK:** [[secr]] requires quoted and large companies to report energy use and emissions, including at least one [[carbon-intensity]] ratio, in their annual accounts. Smaller companies are exempt.

If you are an independent brewery, distillery or brand, you are probably outside all of these. That is not the end of the story.

## The voluntary layer

- **[[b-corp]]** certifies whole-business social and environmental performance, with new standards from 2025.
- **CDP** is the dominant disclosure platform for climate data; suppliers are typically asked to respond by large customers.
- Validated targets via [[science-based-targets]] are increasingly treated as table stakes in supplier assessments.
- Platforms such as EcoVadis score suppliers for procurement teams.

## The layer that actually reaches you: customers

Large retailers, distributors and hospitality groups sit inside CSRD, SECR or their own [[net-zero]] commitments, and your emissions are part of their [[scope-3-emissions|Scope 3]]. So the paperwork rolls downhill as questionnaires, supplier scorecards and data requests for product footprints. In practice this trickle-down, not regulation, is what puts reporting on a small drinks business's desk.

## How a small drinks business should navigate it

1. **Build one dataset, reuse it everywhere.** A [[ghg-protocol]]-based [[carbon-footprint]] (Scopes 1, 2 and material Scope 3) is the common core of CSRD value-chain requests, CDP, B Corp climate questions, SECR and customer questionnaires.
2. **Answer in the customer's format, but keep your own master version,** with base year, methods and evidence documented once.
3. **If you want a framework, pick the lightest that fits:** VSME for EU-facing businesses, B Corp if the whole-business commitment suits your brand.
4. **Do not chase every acronym.** Prioritise the requests tied to revenue, and decline gracefully where a framework demands more than it returns.
5. **Report honestly.** Published claims fall under the [[uk-green-claims-code]], and selective good-news reporting is [[greenwashing]] with a spreadsheet attached.
