**In short:** Extended producer responsibility (EPR) means the businesses that put packaging on the market pay for dealing with it when it becomes waste. The UK's packaging EPR scheme (pEPR) started charging fees in 2025, and from the 2026-27 year your fees depend on how recyclable your packaging is.

## What it is

The principle: shift the cost of collecting and processing packaging waste from council taxpayers to producers, and price packaging so that hard-to-recycle formats cost more. The UK scheme was created by the Producer Responsibility Obligations (Packaging and Packaging Waste) Regulations 2024 and is run by PackUK, the scheme administrator launched in January 2025. Fees apply to household packaging, paid per tonne by material.

## Who it applies to

Jurisdiction: the United Kingdom (all four nations). Large producers, meaning organisations with over 2 million pounds annual turnover that handle over 50 tonnes of packaging a year, report data and pay disposal fees. Smaller producers above 1 million pounds and 25 tonnes report data only. For branded goods the obligation generally sits with the brand owner, so a drinks brand pays for its bottles and cans even if a co-packer fills them. EU note: this is not just a UK idea; every EU member state runs its own EPR scheme (rules now harmonised under the [[eu-packaging-regulation-ppwr]]), so exporters register scheme by scheme.

## Key dates

- 2023: packaging data reporting began.
- 27 June 2025: final Year 1 (2025-26) base fees published, ranging from 192 pounds per tonne for glass to 423 pounds per tonne for plastic; first invoices issued from October 2025.
- From 2025: producers assess packaging recyclability using the Recyclability Assessment Methodology (RAM), which grades each item red, amber or green.
- Year 2 (2026-27): fees become modulated. Red-rated packaging pays about 1.2 times the base fee, green pays less, with the red multiplier rising in later years (towards 2 times by 2028-29). Year 2 illustrative fees were published in December 2025; final Year 2 fees are due to be confirmed in mid-2026.

## Current status

Live and charging. Fee levels have moved several times, so always check the current GOV.UK figures before budgeting.

## What this means for drinks businesses

Packaging-heavy categories feel this hardest. Glass is charged by the tonne and is heavy, so lightweighting bottles cuts fees as well as your [[product-carbon-footprint]]. Getting a green RAM rating (mono-material labels, widely recycled formats, more [[recycled-content]]) now has a direct price. Drinks containers covered by a [[deposit-return-schemes|deposit return scheme]] leave the fee net when DRS launches in October 2027. pEPR sits alongside, not instead of, the [[uk-plastic-packaging-tax]], and both reward the same [[circular-economy]] design choices.
