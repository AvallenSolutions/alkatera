**In short:** The Plastic Packaging Tax (PPT) is a UK tax on plastic packaging that contains less than 30 per cent recycled plastic. From 1 April 2026 the rate is 228.82 pounds per tonne. Use 30 per cent or more recycled plastic and the packaging is not taxed.

## What it is

Introduced by the Finance Act 2021 and in effect since 1 April 2022, PPT is designed to make recycled plastic commercially attractive. It is charged per tonne of finished plastic packaging components that fail the 30 per cent recycled content test. A component counts as plastic if plastic is the heaviest material in it, which catches some items you might not think of as "plastic packaging". The 30 per cent test applies component by component: a bottle, its cap and its label are each assessed separately.

## Who it applies to

Jurisdiction: the United Kingdom. It falls on businesses that manufacture plastic packaging in the UK or import it, including imported packaging that is already filled, so a drinks brand importing finished bottled product is importing taxable packaging. You must register with HMRC once you manufacture or import 10 tonnes or more of plastic packaging in a 12-month period, and you must register and report even if all your packaging meets the 30 per cent threshold and no tax is due. Records proving recycled content are mandatory.

## Key dates

- 1 April 2022: tax introduced at 200 pounds per tonne.
- 1 April 2025: rate rose to 223.69 pounds per tonne.
- 1 April 2026: rate rose to 228.82 pounds per tonne. The rate now rises each April in line with the Consumer Price Index.

## Current status

Fully in force. The rate updates every April, so check GOV.UK for the current figure before pricing decisions. The government has also confirmed plans to allow a mass balance approach for counting chemically recycled plastic, which will matter for food-contact packaging where mechanically recycled material is harder to certify.

## What this means for drinks businesses

PET bottles, shrink wrap, plastic caps and closures, pallet wrap and multipack rings are all in scope. The economics are simple: at 228.82 pounds per tonne, hitting 30 per cent [[recycled-content|recycled content]] usually beats paying the tax, and recycled PET markets have priced this in. The same design choices also cut your fees under [[extended-producer-responsibility]] and prepare you for the EU's 2030 recycled-content minimums under the [[eu-packaging-regulation-ppwr]]. Recycled plastic often (not always) carries a lower [[carbon-footprint]] than virgin, so check your [[emission-factors]] rather than assuming. One caution on marketing: paying or avoiding this tax is not evidence for a green claim; see the [[uk-green-claims-code]] before putting recycled-content percentages on a label, and make sure the number is verifiable.
