**In short:** Double materiality is a way of deciding which sustainability topics a business must report on, by looking in two directions: the impact your business has on people and the environment, and the impact sustainability issues have on your business finances. A topic is material if it matters in either direction.

## The two lenses

- **Impact materiality (inside-out):** where your activities and value chain cause significant actual or potential effects on people or the environment. For a drinks producer that might be water abstraction in a stressed catchment, packaging waste, or emissions across the supply chain.
- **Financial materiality (outside-in):** where sustainability issues create financial risks or opportunities for you. Poor harvests from a changing climate, packaging taxes, energy prices, or losing a listing because a retailer demands emissions data.

Many topics score in both directions, but not all. A business could face little financial risk from a pollution issue it causes, yet still have to report it, and vice versa. That is the point of "double": one lens alone misses half the picture.

## Where it comes from

Double materiality is the foundation of the EU's [[csrd]] reporting law and the [[esrs]] standards that implement it: in-scope companies must run a formal double materiality assessment to decide what they report. The voluntary [[vsme]] standard for smaller businesses deliberately simplifies this. Investor-focused frameworks such as the ISSB use financial materiality only, which is a common source of confusion in [[sustainability-reporting]].

## What this means for drinks businesses

Most independent producers are not directly in scope of CSRD, but large customers are, and their assessments reach into their supply chains: expect questions about your [[carbon-footprint]], [[water-footprint]] and packaging. Running an informal double materiality exercise, even a one-page version, is a sensible way to decide which few topics deserve your limited reporting effort, typically [[drinks-carbon-hotspots|carbon hotspots]], water and packaging before anything else.
