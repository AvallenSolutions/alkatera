**In short:** Recycled content is the proportion of a package made from material that has been used before. It usually cuts the carbon footprint of the pack, and for plastic it now carries direct tax and regulatory consequences.

## PCR and why the definition matters

The gold standard is **post-consumer recycled (PCR)** material: recovered from packaging that consumers actually used and disposed of. Pre-consumer (factory offcut) material is easier to source and less meaningful, since it was never really waste. Credible claims specify PCR percentage and are backed by supplier certificates; vague "made with recycled material" wording invites [[greenwashing]] challenges.

## Why glass, aluminium and PET differ

- **Glass.** Recycled glass (cullet) melts at a lower temperature than virgin raw materials, so every extra portion of cullet saves furnace energy and raw material. But supply is constrained and colour-sorted: green bottles routinely achieve high recycled content, clear (flint) glass much less. Even at high cullet rates, glass remains heavy and energy-intensive, which is why it dominates many drinks footprints (see [[drinks-carbon-hotspots]]).
- **Aluminium.** Remelting aluminium uses around 95% less energy than smelting new metal, so recycled content transforms a can's footprint. Cans are also recycled repeatedly without losing quality. Typical cans already contain substantial recycled metal; the exact share varies by region and supplier.
- **PET plastic.** Food-grade rPET must come from approved recycling processes, supply is tight and it often costs more than virgin resin. EU rules already require PET drinks bottles to contain at least 25% recycled plastic, rising to 30% for all plastic drinks bottles by 2030, and the [[eu-packaging-regulation-ppwr]] extends recycled-content minimums across plastic packaging from 2030.

## Taxes and rules

The [[uk-plastic-packaging-tax]] charges plastic packaging manufactured in or imported into the UK that contains less than 30% recycled plastic, making PCR a direct cost question. [[extended-producer-responsibility]] fee structures and [[deposit-return-schemes]] also reward packs that are easy to recycle back into packs, which is what keeps recycled content available at all: it is one half of the [[circular-economy]] loop.

## What this means for drinks businesses

- Ask suppliers for certified PCR percentages in writing, per SKU, and keep the evidence.
- Use accurate [[emission-factors]] for recycled versus virgin material in your [[product-carbon-footprint]]; the difference is often material.
- For plastic, model the 30% tax threshold explicitly; for glass, remember colour choice affects achievable recycled content.
- Recycled content helps, but weight usually matters more: a lighter pack with average recycled content often beats a heavy one with an impressive percentage.
