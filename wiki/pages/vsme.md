**In short:** The VSME (Voluntary Sustainability Reporting Standard for SMEs) is the EU's official lightweight format for small and medium-sized businesses to report sustainability information. It exists so that an SME can answer every big customer's data request once, in one standard format, instead of filling in a different questionnaire for each.

## What it is

The VSME was developed by EFRAG, the body that also drafts the [[esrs]], and delivered to the European Commission in December 2024. On 30 July 2025 the Commission formally endorsed it through Recommendation (EU) 2025/1710. It is voluntary: no SME is legally required to use it. Its purpose is to give banks, investors and large corporate customers a common, proportionate format for the sustainability data they increasingly request from smaller suppliers.

## What is in it

The standard has two modules:

- **Basic Module:** eleven disclosures covering the essentials, including energy use and greenhouse gas emissions ([[scope-1-emissions|scope 1]] and [[scope-2-emissions|scope 2]]), pollution, water, waste, workforce basics and anti-corruption.
- **Comprehensive Module:** additional disclosures reflecting what larger customers and lenders commonly ask for, such as emissions reduction targets and significant value-chain ([[scope-3-emissions|scope 3]]) emissions.

A small drinks business can start with the Basic Module and add Comprehensive disclosures only where a customer actually needs them.

## Why it matters

The VSME is more than a template. Under the Omnibus amendments to the [[csrd]], companies in scope of mandatory reporting face a "value-chain cap": when gathering data from suppliers with fewer than 1,000 employees, they are not supposed to demand information beyond what the voluntary standard covers. The Commission also plans to anchor a VSME-based standard in law as a delegated act. In practice, that makes the VSME the ceiling for what a supermarket, pub group or global drinks company can reasonably ask of a smaller supplier.

## What this means for drinks businesses

Most breweries, distilleries, wineries and independent brands are exactly who this standard is for. If your buyers, distributors or bank have started asking for a [[carbon-footprint]] or wider ESG data, reporting once in VSME format is usually cheaper than answering bespoke questionnaires, and it signals competence. The hardest part is the emissions data, so start with metered energy and fuel ([[primary-vs-secondary-data|primary data]]) for scope 1 and 2, and build towards product-level numbers with a [[product-carbon-footprint]] where customers want them. It also lays the groundwork if you later pursue [[b-corp]] certification or fuller [[sustainability-reporting]].
