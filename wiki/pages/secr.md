**In short:** SECR is the UK rule that makes quoted and large companies publish their energy use and greenhouse gas emissions in their annual report every year. If your drinks business is large enough to file full accounts with an energy and carbon section, this is why.

## What it is

Streamlined Energy and Carbon Reporting was introduced by 2018 regulations under the Companies Act and applies to financial years starting on or after 1 April 2019. In-scope organisations disclose, in the directors' report: their energy consumption, their [[scope-1-emissions|scope 1]] and [[scope-2-emissions|scope 2]] greenhouse gas emissions, at least one intensity ratio (see [[carbon-intensity]]), the methodology used (most use the [[ghg-protocol]]), and a description of energy-efficiency action taken that year. Quoted companies report global emissions; unquoted companies report UK energy from electricity, gas and transport fuel. Reporting some [[scope-3-emissions|scope 3]] and [[market-based-vs-location-based|market-based figures]] is encouraged but voluntary.

## Who it applies to

Jurisdiction: the United Kingdom. Three groups: quoted companies; unquoted companies that qualify as large under the Companies Act; and large limited liability partnerships. "Large" means meeting at least two of three thresholds, which were raised for financial years beginning on or after 6 April 2025 to: turnover of 54 million pounds or more, balance sheet total of 27 million pounds or more, and 250 or more employees (previously 36 million and 18 million pounds). Organisations using under 40,000 kilowatt hours of energy a year can state that instead of reporting in full.

## Key dates

- 1 April 2019: SECR applies.
- 6 April 2025: Companies Act size thresholds uplifted, taking some businesses out of scope.
- 26 May 2026: the government published its post-implementation review of SECR.

## Current status

In force and unchanged in substance. The May 2026 review concluded SECR should remain, and recommended simplification: clearer requirements, less duplication with other schemes, and possible forward-looking elements. A consultation on streamlining energy and emissions reporting is planned for 2026. Separately, the UK has developed UK Sustainability Reporting Standards (exposure drafts published June 2025), a broader climate disclosure regime whose final scope and timing were still being settled as of mid-2026.

## What this means for drinks businesses

Breweries and distilleries are energy-intensive, so SECR numbers are watched by lenders and buyers, not just filed. Treat it as a floor: the data you gather (metered energy, fuel, an intensity ratio per litre produced or per bottle) is the same [[primary-vs-secondary-data|primary data]] that feeds a full [[carbon-footprint]], customer requests driven by [[csrd]], and target-setting such as [[science-based-targets]]. Done once, well, it serves all of your [[sustainability-reporting]].
