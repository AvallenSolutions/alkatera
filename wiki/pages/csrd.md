**In short:** The CSRD is the European Union law that makes large companies publish audited sustainability information alongside their financial accounts. After the EU's "Omnibus" simplification, it now only applies to companies with more than 1,000 employees, so most drinks businesses will feel it indirectly, through big customers asking them for data.

## What it is

The Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (Directive (EU) 2022/2464) requires in-scope companies to publish a sustainability statement in their annual report, prepared under the [[esrs|European Sustainability Reporting Standards]] and checked by an independent assurance provider. Reports must cover both how sustainability issues affect the company and how the company affects people and planet, a principle called [[double-materiality|double materiality]]. That includes greenhouse gas emissions across [[scope-1-emissions|scope 1]], [[scope-2-emissions|scope 2]] and [[scope-3-emissions|scope 3]].

## Who it applies to

Jurisdiction: the European Union, transposed into each member state's national law. Following the Omnibus amendments, the CSRD applies to EU companies with more than 1,000 employees and net annual turnover above 450 million euros. Listed small and medium-sized companies, originally a third "wave", have been removed from scope entirely. Non-EU parent companies with very large EU turnover are also caught. In the drinks world that means global drinks groups, major retailers and large distributors, not the typical independent brewery, distillery or winery.

## Key dates

- 5 January 2023: CSRD entered into force.
- Financial year 2024: the first wave (large listed companies) reported, publishing in 2025.
- April 2025: the "stop-the-clock" directive delayed the second and third waves by two years.
- 26 February 2026: the Omnibus amending directive was published in the EU Official Journal, confirming the new 1,000-employee and 450 million euro thresholds. First-wave companies that fall below the new thresholds are exempt for financial years 2025 and 2026.
- Financial year 2027: the revised scope applies in full; remaining newly scoped companies report from 2028.

## Current status

In force, but substantially narrowed. The Omnibus changes were formally adopted in February 2026 and member states are now transposing them. The reporting standards themselves are being simplified in parallel; see [[esrs]] for where that stands.

## What this means for drinks businesses

Very few drinks companies are in scope directly. The real effect is the cascade: supermarkets, pub groups and large distributors that must report their [[scope-3-emissions|scope 3 emissions]] will ask suppliers for emissions and other sustainability data. Helpfully, the amended rules include a "value-chain cap": in-scope companies cannot demand more from suppliers with under 1,000 employees than the voluntary [[vsme]] standard covers. Preparing a credible [[product-carbon-footprint]] and basic [[sustainability-reporting|sustainability reporting]] now is the practical way to keep those customer conversations easy. UK-only businesses should also check [[secr]], the separate UK regime.
