**In short:** A deposit return scheme (DRS) adds a small refundable deposit to the price of a drink. Customers get it back when they return the empty container, which pushes collection rates far higher than kerbside recycling. The UK's schemes launch on 1 October 2027, and drinks producers have to register, relabel and charge the deposit.

## What it is

Under a DRS, producers charge the deposit into the supply chain, retailers charge it to shoppers, and return points (often reverse vending machines) refund it. A deposit management organisation runs the money flow and logistics. Countries with mature schemes, such as Germany, Denmark, Finland, the Netherlands and, since February 2024, Ireland, routinely collect around 90 per cent of in-scope containers, and the returned material is clean enough to become high-quality [[recycled-content|recycled content]].

## Who it applies to

Jurisdiction: the United Kingdom (with separate schemes) and, separately, EU member states. In the UK, anyone producing or importing drinks in in-scope containers must register with the scheme administrator, label products correctly, charge the deposit and pay producer fees. Retailers and hospitality venues generally host return points. In the EU, the [[eu-packaging-regulation-ppwr]] requires every member state to run a DRS for single-use plastic bottles and metal drinks containers by 1 January 2029 unless it already collects more than 80 per cent separately, so exporters face a patchwork of national schemes with different labels and deposits.

## Key dates

- June 2025: Scotland aligned its delayed scheme with the England and Northern Ireland framework.
- 1 October 2027: schemes launch across England, Northern Ireland and Scotland, covering PET plastic, steel and aluminium containers from 150 millilitres to 3 litres. Glass is excluded. The deposit management organisation trades as Exchange for Change.
- 1 October 2027: Wales launches its own scheme on the same date for PET and metal, under regulations laid in February 2026. Wales also brings glass into scope, phased in through a transition period during which glass carries a zero-pence deposit.
- 1 January 2029: EU-wide DRS obligation under the PPWR.

## Current status

UK legislation is in place and scheme build-out is under way; deposit levels and detailed producer fees are set by the scheme administrators, so watch their announcements through 2026 and 2027.

## What this means for drinks businesses

If you sell canned or PET-packaged drinks in the UK, plan for registration, new on-pack labelling, deposit accounting in your pricing, and separate handling for Wales once glass phases in. Containers in a DRS leave the disposal-fee net of [[extended-producer-responsibility|packaging EPR]], which softens the cost. Higher-quality returned PET should also ease compliance with the [[uk-plastic-packaging-tax]] and EU recycled-content targets. DRS is one of the few policies that genuinely closes the loop, a working piece of the [[circular-economy]] rather than an aspiration.
