## Article 2

**Title:** Glass vs aluminium vs kegs: the carbon cost of drinks packaging
**Suggested slug:** `glass-vs-aluminium-vs-kegs-packaging-carbon`
**Meta description:** A data-led comparison of the carbon footprint of glass,
aluminium, PET, and kegs for drinks brands, and how to choose the lower-impact

**Tags:** packaging, carbon, benchmarks, LCA

### TL;DR
For most drinks, packaging is one of the two largest sources of carbon. Glass is
typically the heaviest per serving; aluminium cans and reusable kegs usually cut
that substantially, but the answer depends on recycled content, transport
distance, and reuse rates. ⚠️[VERIFY: indicative per-unit figures].

### The variables that change the answer
- **Recycled content:** recycled glass and aluminium can roughly halve

  cans.
