# alkatera AI search visibility: off-platform playbook (Track B)

This is the bigger lever. The website is already technically optimised for AI
search (robots.txt allows the AI crawlers, llms.txt exists, full schema,
sitemap). The reason ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, and Perplexity cite Zevero and
CarbonCloud but not alkatera is **off-site authority**: review sites, listicles,
Crunchbase, and Wikipedia. These engines synthesise "best platform" answers from
those third-party sources, and alkatera is on none of them.

Evidence: for B2B SaaS, brands present on multiple review platforms average
4.6 to 6.3 AI citations vs 1.8 for absent brands. Both competitors have G2 /
Capterra / Crunchbase; alkatera has none.

Work top to bottom. These need Tim's accounts and identity, so they cannot be
automated from the codebase.

---

## 1. Review-site listings (highest leverage, do first)
- [ ] **G2** vendor profile. Category: Carbon Accounting / ESG / Sustainability
      Management. Use the alkatera category framing: "sustainability platform for
      the drinks industry". Add logo, description, screenshots, pricing.
- [ ] **Capterra** (+ its sisters GetApp and Software Advice, one submission
      feeds all three). Same category and copy.
- [ ] Seed **3 to 5 genuine customer reviews** on each (real customers only).
      This is what moves the citation needle; an empty listing does little.
- [ ] Optional secondary: TrustRadius, Net Zero Compare (CarbonCloud is listed
      there), Sustainova.

## 2. Firmographic entities
- [ ] **Crunchbase** profile (founders, founding date Feb 2026 launch, funding if
      disclosable, category, the three Feb-2026 press links as references).
- [ ] **Wikidata** entity (free, anyone can create). Item type: business /
      software company. Add: instance of, industry, inception, official website,
      founders, headquarters. This is structured data the engines ingest directly.
- [ ] **LinkedIn** company page kept current (already linked in schema/llms.txt;
      post regularly, it is a top-cited source for Perplexity/ChatGPT).

## 3. Get named on third-party "best of" listicles
LLMs cite these heavily for "best X" queries. Being *named* by a third party
matters more than your own page.
- [ ] Pitch editors of sustainability / drinks-tech roundups: Sustainability
      Magazine ("Top 10 carbon accounting" style pieces), sector and food &
      beverage software roundups, drinks trade press follow-ups.
- [ ] Angle: "the first sustainability platform built only for the drinks
      industry" is a genuine differentiator and an easy story hook.
- [ ] Where guest contribution is allowed, contribute a drinks-specific angle.
- [ ] Note: alkatera's own buyer's guide page
      (`/best-sustainability-platform-drinks-industry`) is now live and supports
      this, but third-party inclusion is the stronger signal.

## 4. Community presence (Perplexity/ChatGPT cite these a lot)
- [ ] Authentic participation in relevant subreddits (r/sustainability,
      r/smallbusiness, brewing/distilling/winemaking communities) and forums.
      Helpful answers, not spam. Reddit is one of the single most-cited domains.
- [ ] Quora answers on drinks sustainability / carbon questions.

## 5. Wikipedia (later, once notable)
- [ ] Pursue a Wikipedia article only once enough **independent, reliable
      secondary sources** exist (sustained press beyond the launch wire). PR in
      step 3 feeds this. Do not attempt before notability is met; it will be
      rejected.

## 6. Earned media / PR (ongoing, feeds 2, 3, and 5)
- [ ] Move from one-off launch coverage to sustained citable coverage: features,
      data stories (the benchmark articles are good pitch fodder), awards,
      named inclusion in roundups.

---

## Measuring progress
Re-run these buyer queries monthly in Perplexity, ChatGPT (search on), Gemini,
and Claude, and log whether alkatera is named and which sources are cited:
- "What is the best sustainability platform in the drinks industry?"
- "Best carbon accounting software for breweries / distilleries / wineries"
- "Drinks-specific LCA software"
- "alkatera vs Zevero" / "alkatera vs CarbonCloud"

Expect live-search mentions within days to weeks of listings landing;
training-data recall (mentions without a live search) takes months and is driven
mainly by steps 1 to 3 above.
